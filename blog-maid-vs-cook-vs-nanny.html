Maid vs Cook vs Nanny: Which Home Help Do You Actually Need?

April 10, 2026 6 min read

One of the most common questions we get from families is: "I need help at home, but I'm not sure what kind." Should you hire a part-time maid? A full-time cook? A nanny? Or maybe a live-in helper who does everything?

Let's break down each type so you can make the right choice.

1. Part-Time Maid

What they do: Cleaning, mopping, dishes, laundry, and light kitchen work.

Ideal for: Working professionals and couples who need daily upkeep but don't require full-day help. Most common in urban apartments.

Typical schedule: Morning (7-11 AM) or split shift (7-9 AM + 5-7 PM).

2. Specialized Cook

What they do: Meal planning, preparation, and kitchen management tailored to your dietary preferences.

Ideal for: Families who value nutrition, have dietary restrictions, or simply don't have time to cook. Especially popular with health-conscious professionals and families with specific cuisine needs (Jain, diabetic-friendly, etc.).

💡 Tip: When hiring a cook, always conduct a trial cooking session. Ask them to prepare 2-3 dishes from your regular menu to assess taste, hygiene, and speed.

3. Nanny / Babysitter

What they do: Childcare, feeding, bathing, school pickup/drop, play supervision, and developmental activities.

Ideal for: Dual-income families or single parents with children under 10. A good nanny becomes an extension of the family.

Critical requirement: Patience, first-aid knowledge, and genuine affection for children. Always prioritize personality over price.

4. Elderly Caregiver

What they do: Mobility assistance, medication reminders, meal prep, companionship, and emergency response.

Ideal for: NRI families with parents in India, or working professionals who can't be home during the day. Increasingly in demand across India.

5. Live-In Helper

What they do: Complete household management — cleaning, cooking, laundry, childcare, and general upkeep.

Ideal for: Large families, joint families, or homes with multiple needs. The all-in-one solution.

Note: Live-in helpers require accommodation (a separate room or at least a private sleeping area), meals, and one weekly day off.

How to Decide?

Ask yourself these 3 questions:

  • What's my biggest pain point? Cleaning → Maid. Cooking → Cook. Kids → Nanny.
  • How many hours of help do I need? Under 6 hours → Part-time. Over 8 → Full-time or live-in.
  • What's my budget? Start with one role and expand as needed.
